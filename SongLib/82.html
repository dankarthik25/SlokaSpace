Text | 1 |

udyaddinadyutimindukirīṭāṁ
tuṅgakucāṁ nayanatrayayuktām |
smēramukhīṁ varadāṅkuśapāśāṁ
’bhītikarāṁ prabhajē bhuvanēśīm ||1||

Synonyms

udyat:Rising ; dina:Day ; dyuti:Splendour, Brightness, Lustre ; indu:Moon ; kirīṭa:a Diadem, Crest, any Ornament used as a Crown, Tiara ; udyaddinadyutimindukirīṭāṁ:Who has the Splendour of the Rising Sun and Holds the Moon on Her Crown ;
tuṅga:Lofty, High ; kuca:Bosom ; tuṅgakucāṁ:Who has High Breasts ; nayana:Eye ; traya:Triple ; yukta:Yoked, Joined, United ; nayanatrayayuktām:Who has Three Eyes ;
smēra:Smiling ; mukha:Chief, Principal, Best ; smēramukhīṁ:Who has a Smiling Face ; vara:Best, Excellent ; dā:Give ; aṅkuśa:Hook ; pāśa:Noose ; varadāṅkuśapāśāṁ:Who shows the Boon-Giving gesture and holds the Ankusha and Pasha ;
abhīti:Fearlessness ; kara:Act of Doing ; ’bhītikarāṁ:Who shows the gesture of Fearlessness ; prabhaj:to Honour ; prabhajē:Salutations ; bhuvana:World ; īśa:Lord ; bhuvanēśīm:Devi Bhuvaneswari, the Goddess of the World ;

Translation

(Salutations to Devi Bhuvaneshwari) Who has the Splendour of the Rising Sun of the Day, and Who holds the Moon on Her Crown like an Ornament.
Who has High Breasts and Three Eyes (containing the Sun, Moon and the Fire),
Who has a Smiling Face and shows the Vara Mudra (Boon-Giving gesture), holds an Ankusha (a Hook) and a Pasha (a Noose), ...
1.4 ... and displays the Abhaya Mudra (gesture of Fearlessness) with Her Hands; Salutations to Devi Bhuvaneshwari.

Text | 2 |

sindūr[a]-āruṇa-vigrah[a]-āṁ tri-nayanāṁ māṇikya-mauli-sphurat
tārā-nāyaka-śēkharāṁ smita-mukhīm-ā-pīna-vakṣ[as]o-ruhām |
pāṇi-bhyām-ali-pūrṇa-ratna-caṣakaṁ raktotpalaṃ vibhratīṃ
saumyāṁ ratna-ghaṭastha-madhy[a]-acaraṇāṁ dyāyēt-parām-ambikām ||

Synonyms

sindūra:Vermilion ; aruṇa:Dawn,Colour of Morning,Reddish-Brown ; vigraha:having a handsome Form or Shape, Beautiful ; sindūrāruṇavigrahāṁ:Whose Beautiful Form has the Reddish Glow of the Early Morning Sun ; tri:Three ; nayana:Eye ; trinayanāṁ:Who has Three Eyes ; māṇikya:Ruby ; mauli:Hair Ornamented and Braided around the head ; sphur:To throb, Flash, Break Forth, Evident or Manifest ; māṇikyamaulisphurat:Whose Head Glitters with the Ornament of Gems ;
tārā:a fixed Star ; nāyaka:Leader,Hero of the Dance ; śēkhara:Peak ; tārānāyakaśēkharāṁ:Who holds the Chief of the Stars [i.e. Moon] on Her Head ; smita:Smiled, Smiling ; mukha:Chief, Principal, Best ; pīna:Swelling, Swollen, Full, Round, Large ; vakṣas:Breast, Bosom, Chest ; smitamukhīmāpīnavakṣoruhām:Who has a Smiling Face and Full Bosom ;
pāṇi:Hand ; ali:Bee ; pūrṇa:Filled, Full ; ratna:Gem ; caṣaka:Cup ; pāṇibhyāmalipūrṇaratnacaṣakaṁ:Who [holds] a Gem-studded Cup filled with Divine Liquor on Her Hands ; bhr̥:To Bear, Carry ; saṁvibhratīṁ:Holds [the Cup of Divine Liquor] ; śāśvata:Eternal, Perpetual ; śāśvatīṁ:Who is Eternal ;
saumya:Cool, Placid, Gentle, Mild, Happy, Cheerful ; saumyāṁ:Who is Cool and Joyful ; ratna:Gem ; ghaṭa:Jar, Pitcher ; madhya:Middle ; caraṇa:Feet ; ratnaghaṭasthamadhyacaraṇāṁ:Whose Feet rests on a Pitcher filled with Gems ; dhyāna:Meditation ; para:Following, Succeeding, Highest, Supreme ; ambikā:Mother ; dyāyētparāmambikām:[We] Meditate on the Supreme Mother ;

Translation

I mediate upon Saffron-Reddish Form Glow of the Early Morning Sun; Who has Three Eyes and Whose Head Glitters with the Ornament of Gems,
Who Holds the Chief of Star (i.e. the Moon) on Her Head, Who has a Smiling Face and Full Bosom,
Who Holds a Gem-studded Cup filled with Divine Liquor on Her Hands, and Who is Eternal,
Who is Cool and Joyful, and rests Her Feet on a Pitcher filled with Jewels; We Meditate on the Supreme Ambika (Supreme Mother).
