kausalyā suprajā rāma pūrvāsandhyā pravartatē |
uttiṣṭha naraśārdūla karttavyaṁ daivamāhnikam ||1||

Synonyms

kausalya:Kaushalya, the Mother of Sri Ramachandra ; kausalyā:Kaushalya, the Mother of Sri Ramachandra ; su:Good, Excellent, Virtuous, Beautiful ; prajā:Offspring, Children ; suprajā:The Excellent and Beautiful Child [of Kaushalya] ; rāma:Rama ; pūrvā:East ; sandhyā:Juncture of Day and Night ; pūrvāsandhyā:In the East in this Juncture of Night and Day [Dawn] ; pra:Prefix usually meaning forth, away ; vr̥t:Turn, Advance ; pravartatē:[Dawn] is Advancing [Night turning into Day] ;

Translation

(Salutations to Sri Govinda) O Rama, the most Excellent Son of Kaushalya; in the East the Dawn is fast approaching in this Beautiful juncture of Night and Day,
Please Wake Up in Our Hearts, O Purushottama (the Best of Men ) so that we can perform our Daily Duties as Divine Rituals unto You and thus do the Ultimate Duty of our lives.

(Salutations to Sri Govinda) In this Beautiful Dawn the Sun is rising and the Lotus Flowers are blossoming,
