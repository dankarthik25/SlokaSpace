Text | 1 |

narasimha-kavacaṁ vakṣye
prahlādenoditaṁ purā
sarva-rakṣā-karaṁ puṇyaṁ
sarvopadrava-nāśanam

Translation

I shall now recite the Narasimha-kavaca,
formerly spoken by Prahlada Maharaja.
It is most pious, vanquishes all kinds of impediments,
and provides one all protection.

Text | 2 |

sarva-sampat-karaṁ caiva
svarga-mokṣa-pradāyakam
dhyātvā nṛsiṁhaṁ deveśaṁ
hema-siṁhāsana-sthitam

Synonyms

dhyātvā:by meditating, after thinking, having fixed the mind in meditation ;

Translation

It bestows upon one all opulences and
can give one elevation to the heavenly planets or liberation.
One should meditate on Lord Narasimha,
Lord of the universe, seated upon a golden throne.

Text | 3 |

vivṛtāsyaṁ tri-nayanaṁ
śarad-indu-sama-prabham
lakṣmyāliṅgita-vāmāṅgam
vibhūtibhir upāśritam

Synonyms

vivṛta:visible, revealed ; āsyam:mouth, face, the mouth, having a mouth in which, whose face ;
indu:like the full moon ; sama:exactly like that of ; prabhām:effulgent form ;

Translation

His mouth is wide open, He has three eyes, and
He is as radiant as the autumn moon.
He is embraced by Lakshmidevi on his left side, and
His form is the shelter of all opulences, both material and spiritual.

Text | 4 |

catur-bhujaṁ komalāṅgaṁ
svarṇa-kuṇḍala-śobhitam

Synonyms

komala-aṅgaḥ:with a body as soft as a lotus flower ;
shobitha:Graceful ;
saroja:a lotus ; uraskam:whose chest ;
keyūra:ornaments, bracelets, jeweled necklace, armlets ;

Translation

The Lord has four arms, and His limbs are very soft.
He is decorated with golden earrings.
His chest is resplendent like the lotus flower, and
His arms are decorated with jewel-studded ornaments.

Text | 5 |

tapta-kāncana-sankāśaṁ
pīta-nirmala-vāsasam
indrādi-sura-mauliṣṭha
sphuran māṇikya-dīptibhiḥ

Synonyms

tapta-kancana:gold purified with fire ;
pīta:yellow ; nirmala:spotless pure very clearly ; vāsāḥ:whose clothing, dressed those who live ;
sphuran:glittering ; dipti:splendour , lustre, brightness, lucent ;

Translation

He is dressed in a spotless yellow garment,
which exactly resembles molten gold.
He is the original cause of existence, beyond the mundane sphere, for the great demigods headed by Indra.
He appears bedecked with rubies which are blazingly effulgent.

Text | 6 |

Translation

His two feet are very attractive, and
He is armed with various weapons such as the conch, disc, etc.
Garuda joyfully offers prayers
with great reverence.

Text | 7 |

Having seated Lord Narasimhadeva upon the lotus of one``s heart,
one should recite the following mantra:
May Lord Narasimha, protect my head.
who protects all the planetary systems,

Text | 8 |

sarvago ’pi stambha-vāsaḥ
phālaṁ me rakṣatu dhvanim
nṛsiṁho me dṛśau pātu
soma-sūryāgni-locanaḥ

Translation

Although the Lord is all-pervading, He hid Himself within a pillar.
May He protect my speech and the results of my activities.
May Lord Narasimha, protect my eyes,
whose eyes are the sun, and fire.

Text | 9 |

smṛtiṁ me pātu nṛhariḥ
muni-varya-stuti-priyaḥ
nāsāṁ me siṁha-nāśas tu
mukhaṁ lakṣmī-mukha-priyaḥ

Synonyms

smrtam:memory ; nasam:nose ;

Translation

May Lord Nrhari, protect my memory.
who is pleased by the prayers offered by the best of sages,
May He who has the nose of a lion protect my nose, and
May He whose face is very dear to the goddess of fortune protect my mouth.

Text | 10 |

sarva-vidyādhipaḥ pātu
nṛsiṁho rasanām mama
vaktraṁ pātv indu-vadanaḥ
sadā prahlāda-vanditaḥ

Synonyms

pah:leader ;
rasanam:the sense of taste(tongue) ;
vaktram:face, mouth, head ; indu-vadanam:moon like face ;

Translation

May Lord Narasimha, who is the knower of all sciences,
protect my sense of taste.
May He whose face is beautiful as the full moon and
who is offered prayers by Prahlada Maharaja protect my face.

Text | 11 |

nṛsiṁhah pātu me kaṇṭhaṁ
skandhau bhū-bharaṇānta-kṛt
divyāstra-śobhita-bhujo
nṛsiṁhaḥ pātu me bhujau

Synonyms

skandhau:shoulder,trunk,branch ;
krt:created,doing, performing,mainter, sustainer ;

Translation

May Lord Narasimha protect my throat.
He is the sustainer of the earth and the performer of unlimitedly wonderful activities.
May He protect my shoulders. His arms are resplendent with transcendental weapons.
May He protect my shoulders.

Text | 12 |

nṛsiṁhaḥ pātu sarvataḥ
hṛdayaṁ yogi-sādhyaś
ca nivāsaṁ pātu me hariḥ

Synonyms

karau:hands,doing, bestows ;
sarvatah:all sides ;

Translation

May the Lord, who bestows benedictions upon the demigods, protect my hands, and
May Lord (He) protect me from all sides.
May He who is achieved by the perfect yogis protect my heart, and
May Lord Hari protect my dwelling place.

Text | 13 |

madhyaṁ pātu hiraṇyāksa-
vakṣaḥ-kukṣi-vidāraṇaḥ
nābhiṁ me pātu nṛhariḥ
sva-nābhi-brahma-saṁstutaḥ

Synonyms

vaksha:chest ; kukshi:belly,abdomen ; vidaranah:tears or ripped ;
samstutah:properly glorified ;

Translation

May Lord (He) who ripped apart the chest and abdomen of the great demon Hiranyaksha
May Lord (He) protect my waist, and
May Lord Nrhari protect my navel.
The Lord who offered prayers by Lord Brahma, who has sprung from his own navel.

Text | 14 |

brahmāṇḍa-koṭayaḥ kaṭyāṁ
yasyāsau pātu me kaṭim
guhyaṁ me pātu guhyānāṁ

Synonyms

brahmanda koṭayaḥ:ten millions ; katyam:waist,, on his hip ;
yasyasau:yasya(one whose) ; patu me katim:waist, hip ;

Translation

May He on whose hips rest all the universes protect my hips.
May the Lord protect my private parts.
He is the knower of all mantras and all mysteries, but He Himself is not visible.

Text | 15 |

jānunī nara-rūpa-dhṛk
jaṅghe pātu dharā-bhāra
-hartā yo ’sau nṛ-keśarī

Translation

May He who is the original Cupid protect my thighs.
May He who exhibits a human-like form protect my knees.
May the remover of the burden of the earth, who appears in a form which is half-man and half-lion, protect my calves.

Text | 16 |

pādau me nṛharīśvaraḥ
sahasra-śīrṣā-puruṣaḥ
pātu me sarvaśas tanum

Translation

May the bestower of heavenly opulence,
May Lord protect my feet, who is the Supreme Controller in the form of a man and lion combined.
May the thousand-headed Supreme enjoyer
may protect my body from all sides and in all respects.

Text | 17 |

mahograḥ pūrvataḥ pātu
mahā-vīrāgrajo ’gnitaḥ
mahā-viṣṇuḥ dakṣiṇe tu
mahā-jvālas tu nairṛtau

Translation

May that most ferocious personality protect me from the east.
May He who is superior to the greatest heroes protect me from the southeast, which is presided over by Agni.
May the Supreme Vishnu protect me from the south, and
May that person of blazing luster protect me from the southwest.

Text | 18 |

paścime pātu sarveśo
diśi me sarvatomukhaḥ
nṛsiṁhaḥ pātu vāyavyāṁ
saumyāṁ bheeṣaṇa-vigrahaḥ

Translation

May the Lord of everything protect me from the west. His faces are everywhere,
May the Lord protect me from this direction.
May Lord Narasimha protect me from the northwest, which is predominated by Vayu, and
May He whose form is in itself the supreme ornament protect me from the north, where Soma resides.

Text | 19 |

sarva-maṅgala-dāyakaḥ
saṁsāra-bhayadaḥ pātu
mṛtyor mṛtyur nṛ-keśarī

Translation

May the all-auspicious Lord, who Himself bestows all-auspiciousness, protect from the northeast, the direction of the sun-god, and
May He who is death personified protect me from fear of death and rotation in this material world.

Text | 20 |

prahlāda-mukha-maṅḍitam
bhaktimān yaḥ paṭhennityam
sarva-pāpaiḥ pramucyate

Translation

This Narasimha-kavaca
has been ornamented by issuing from the mouth of Prahlada Maharaja.
Any devotee who reads continuously becomes
freed from all sins.

Text | 21 |

One can have wealth, many sons, and a long life.
Whatever one desires in this world he can attain without doubt.

Text | 22 |

sarvatra vijayī bhavet
bhūmy antarīkṣa-divyānāṁ
grahānāṁ vinivāraṇam

Translation

One can becomes victorious who desires victory, and
One can indeed becomes a conqueror.
One can travell to all influence planets, earthly, heavenly, and everything in between.

Text | 23 |

This is the supreme remedy for the poisonous effects of serpents and scorpions, and

Text | 24 |

Translation

One may write this most auspicious prayer on his arm, or inscribe it on a palm-leaf and
attach it to his wrist, and all his activities will become perfect.

Text | 26 |

This prayer is most auspicious of all auspicious things, and

Text | 27 |

mantra-siddhiḥ prajāyate
anena mantra-rājena
kṛtvā bhasmābhi maṅtraṇam

Translation

This Kavaca-mantra is the king of all mantras. One attains by it what would be attained by anointing oneself with ashes and chanting all other mantras.

Text | 28 |

tasya gṛaha-bhayaṁ haret
tri-vāraṁ japamānas tu
dattaṁ vāryābhimantrya ca

Translation

Having marked ones body with tilaka, taking acamana with water, and
reciting this mantra three times, one will find that the fear of all inauspicious planets is removed.

Text | 29 |

Translation

That person who recites this mantra, meditating upon Lord Narasimhadeva,

Text | 30 |

kimatra bahunoktena
nṛsimha sadṛśo bhavet
manasā cintitam yattu
sa tacchāpnotya samśayaṁ

Translation

Why should more be said? One acquires qualitative oneness with Narasimha himself.
There is no doubt that the desires in the mind of one who meditates will be granted.

Text | 31 |

garjantaṁ garjayantam nija-bhuja-patalaṁ sphoṭayantaṁ hatantaṁ
dipyantaṁ tāpayantaṁ divi bhuvi ditijaṁ kṣepayantam kṣipantam
krandantaṁ roṣayantaṁ diśi diśi satataṁ saṁharantaṁ bharantaṁ
vīkṣantaṁ ghūrṇayantaṁ kara-nikara-śataiḥ divya-siṁhaṁ namāmi

Translation

Lord Narasimha roars loudly and causes others to roar. With His multitudes of arms He tears the demons asunder and kills them in this way.
He is always seeking out and tormenting the demoniac descendants of Diti, both on this earth planet and in the higher planets, and
He throws them down and scatters them. He cries with great anger as He destroys the demons in all directions, yet
with His unlimited hands He sustains, protects, and nourishes the cosmic manifestation. I offer my respectful obeisances to the Lord, who has assumed the form of a transcendental lion.

Text | 32 |

iti śrī-brahmāṇḍa-purāṇe prahlādoktaṁ
śrī-nṛsiṁha-kavacaṁ sampūrṇam

Translation

Thus ends the Narasimha-kavaca as it is described by Prahlada Maharaja in the Brahmanda Purana.
