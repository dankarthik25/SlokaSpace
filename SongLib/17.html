Text | 1 |

(prabhu he!)
emona durmati, saḿsāra bhitore,
poḍiyā āchinu āmi
tava nija-jana, kono mahājane,
pāṭhāiyā dile tumi

Synonyms

prabhu he!:O Lord! ; emona:this ; durmati:wicked mind ; saḿsāra:material world ; bhitore:within ; poḍiyā:falling ; āchinu:am ; āmi:I ; tava:Your ; nija jana:[Your] own people ; kono:some ; mahājane:great exalted personalities ; pāṭhāiyā:sending ; dile:gave ;

Translation

1) Oh Lord! Such a wicked mind has brought me into this world, but one of Your pure and elevated devotees has come to bring me out of it.

Text | 2 |

doyā kori’ more, patita dekhiyā,
kohilo āmāre giyā
ohe dīna-jana, śuno bhālo kathā,
ullasita ha’be hiyā

Synonyms

doyā:mercy ; koriyā:doing ; more:upon me ; patita:fallen ; dekhiyā:seeing ; kohilo:he said ; āmāre:to me ; giyā:going ; śuno:listen ; bhālo:good ; kathā:facts ; ullasita:enthusiastic ; habe:will be ; hiyā:heart ;

Translation

2) He saw me so fallen and wretched, took pity, and came to me saying, “O humbled soul, please listen to this good tiding, for it will gladden your heart.

Text | 3 |

tomāre tārite, śrī-kṛṣṇa-caitanya,
navadwīpe avatār
tomā heno koto, dīna hīna jane,
korilena bhava-pār

Synonyms

tomāre:to you ; tārite:delivering ; śrī-kṛṣṇa-caitanya:Lord Sri Krsna Caitanya ; navadwīpe:in Navadvipa ; avatār:incarnation ; tomā:to you ; heno:as in ; koto:how many ; dīna hīna:fallen and destitute ; jane:people ; korilena:please do ; bhava:ocean of misery ; pār:cross ;

Translation

“Sri Krsna Caitanya has appeared in the land of Navadvipa to deliver you. He has safely conducted many miserable souls such as you across the sea of worldly existence.”

Text | 4 |

vedera pratijñā, rākhibāra tare,
rukma-varna vipra-suta
mahāprabhu nāme, nadīyā mātāya,
sańge bhāi avadhūta

Synonyms

vedera:of the Vedas ; pratijñā:promise ; rākhibāra:protector of ; varna:color ; vipra:brahmana ; suta:son ; mahāprabhu:Mahaprahu (lit. Great Lord) ; nāme:name ; nadīyā:Nadiya ; mātāya:intoxicated ; sańge:in association ; bhāi:brother ; avadhūta:a name of Lord Nityananda (lit. the religious madman) ;

Translation

4) “To fulfill the promise of the Vedas, the son of a brahmana bearing the name Mahaprabhu of golden complexion, has descended with His brother the avadhuta Nityananda. Together They have overwhelmed all of Nadia with divine ecstasy.”

Text | 5 |

nanda-suta jini, caitanya gosāi,
nija-nāma kori’ dān
tārilo jagat, tumi-o jāiyā,
loho nija-paritrāṇ

Synonyms

nanda suta:son of Nanda Maharaja ; jini:whose ; caitanya:Lord Caitanya Mahaprabhu ; gosāñi:goswami ; nija:ones own ; nāma:name ; kori(yā):doing ; dān:giving ; tārilo:delivered ; jagat:the world ; tumi:you ; o:also ; jāiyā:go ; loho:take ; nija:ones own ; paritrāṇ:deliverance ;

Translation

“Sri Caitanya, who is Krishna Himself, the son of Nanda, has saved the world by freely distributing His own holy name. Go also and receive your deliverance.”

Text | 6 |

se kathā śuniyā, āsiyāchi, nātha!
tomāra caraṇa-tale
bhakativinoda,kāńdiyā kāńdiyā,
āpana-kāhinī bole

Synonyms

se:this ; kathā:words ; śuniyā:listening ; āsiyāchi:I am coming ; tomāra:Your ; caraṇa:Lotus feet ; tale:soles of the feet ; bhakativinoda:Bhaktivinoda Thakura ; kāńdiyā kāńdiyā:crying and crying ; āpana:my own ; kāhinī:story ; bole:says ;

Translation

6) O Lord, hearing those words, Bhaktivinoda has come weeping to the soles of Your lotus feet and tells the story of his life.
