Text | 1 |

akrodha paramānanda nityānanda rāya
abhimāna śūnya nitāi nagare beḍāya

Synonyms

akrodha:not angry ; paramānanda:supreme bliss ; nityānanda rāya:Lord Nityananda ; abhimāna:false ego ; śūnya:void ; nitāi:Lord Nityananda ; nagare:in the villages ; beḍāya:wanders ;

Translation

1) The noble Lord Nityananda is never angry, for He is the personification of supreme transcendental bliss. Devoid of any concept of false ego, Nitai wanders about the town.

Text | 2 |

adhama patita jīvera dvāre dvāre giyā
harināma mahā-mantra dicchena bilāiyā

Synonyms

adhama:wretched ; patita:the destitute ; jīvera:of the souls ; dvāre dvāre:door to door ; giyā:having gone ; harināma mahā-mantra:the great chanting of the Holy Names ; dicchena:He gives ; bilāiyā:distributing ;

Translation

Going from door to door in the residences of the most fallen and wretched souls, He freely distributes the gift of the Hari-nama mahamantra.

Text | 3 |

jā’re dekhe tā’re kahe dante tṛṇa dhari’
‘āmāre kiniyā laha bala gaurahari’

Synonyms

jāre dekhe:whoever He sees ; tāre kahe:to them He says ; āmāre kiniyā:purchasing Me ; laha:please take ; bala:say ; gaurahari:Lord Caitanya ;

Translation

3) He exclaims to whomever he sees while holding straw between his teeth, “Please purchase me by worshipping Gaurahari!”

Text | 4 |

eta bali’ nityānanda bhūme gaḍi’ jāya
sonāra parvata jena dhūlāte loṭāya

Synonyms

eta:thus ; bali:having said ; nityānanda:Lord Nityananda ; bhūme:on the ground ; gaḍi jāya:rolls about ; sonāra parvata:the mountain of gold ; jena:just like ; dhūlāte loṭāya:tumbling in the dust ;

Translation

Saying thus, Nityananda Prabhu rolls about on the ground, appearing like a golden mountain tumbling in the dust.

Text | 5 |

hena avatāre jā’ra rati nā janmila
locana bale sei pāpī ela āra gela

Synonyms

hena avatāre:for such an incarnation ; jāra rati:whose affection ; nā janmila:has not come about ; locana:Locana dasa Thakura ; bale:says ; sei pāpī:that sinful person ; ela:comes ; āra:and ; gela:goes (in repeated birth and death). ;

Translation

Locana Dasa Thakura says, “Whoever has not experienced the awakening of affection for such an avatara as this, that sinful person simply comes and goes uselessly in the cycle of repeated birth and death.”
